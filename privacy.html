Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 2025

Your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Policy explains how Tribal Installment Loans collects, uses, discloses, and otherwise processes personal information in connection with our websites, mobile applications, and other online services that link to this Privacy Policy (collectively, the "Services").

1. Introduction

Tribal Installment Loans ("we," "us," "our," or "Company") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy describes the types of information we collect from you when you visit our website, use our mobile applications, or interact with our services, how we use that information, and the choices you have regarding your information.

Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. By accessing or using our Services, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by all the terms of this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use our Services.

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or applicable laws. We will notify you of any material changes by posting the updated Privacy Policy on our website and updating the "Last Updated" date. Your continued use of our Services after any modifications indicates your acceptance of the updated Privacy Policy.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

We collect information that you provide directly to us, including:

  • Application Information: When you apply for a loan, we collect personal information such as your name, date of birth, Social Security number, contact information (email address, phone number, mailing address), employment information, income details, and financial information.
  • Account Information: When you create an account on our platform, we collect login credentials, security questions and answers, and profile information.
  • Communication Information: When you contact us via email, phone, chat, or other communication channels, we collect the content of your communications, contact information, and any attachments you provide.
  • Payment Information: When you make payments or set up automatic payments, we collect bank account information, payment method details, and transaction history.
  • Documentation: We may collect copies of identification documents, proof of income, bank statements, and other financial documents necessary to verify your identity and assess your loan application.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you access or use our Services, we automatically collect certain information about your device and usage patterns, including:

  • Device Information: Device type, operating system, unique device identifiers, mobile network information, and device settings.
  • Usage Information: Pages visited, links clicked, time spent on pages, search queries, referral source, and other usage patterns.
  • Location Information: We may collect approximate location information based on IP address or, with your permission, precise location information through GPS or Bluetooth technologies.
  • Log Information: Server logs containing IP address, browser type, access times, and pages viewed.
  • Cookies and Similar Technologies: Information collected through cookies, web beacons, pixels, and similar tracking technologies.

2.3 Information from Third Parties

We may receive information about you from third parties, including:

  • Credit reporting agencies and credit bureaus
  • Employers and employment verification services
  • Financial institutions and banking partners
  • Fraud prevention and identity verification services
  • Marketing and analytics partners
  • Social media platforms (if you connect your account)
  • Other lenders and financial service providers

4. Cookies and Similar Technologies

4.1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit our website or use our Services. These files contain information that helps us recognize you and remember your preferences when you return to our Services.

4.2 Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: These cookies are necessary for the basic functionality of our Services, such as authentication, security, and session management.
  • Performance Cookies: These cookies help us understand how you use our Services and allow us to analyze usage patterns and improve performance.
  • Functional Cookies: These cookies enable enhanced features and personalization, such as remembering your preferences and settings.
  • Marketing Cookies: These cookies track your activity across websites to deliver targeted advertisements and measure marketing campaign effectiveness.
  • Third-Party Cookies: Third-party service providers may place cookies on your device to provide analytics, advertising, and other services.

4.3 Similar Tracking Technologies

In addition to cookies, we use other tracking technologies, including:

  • Web Beacons: Small transparent images embedded in web pages and emails that track user interactions.
  • Pixels: Tracking pixels that record user actions and engagement.
  • Local Storage: Data stored locally on your device that persists across browser sessions.
  • Mobile Identifiers: Unique identifiers on mobile devices used for tracking and personalization.

4.4 Managing Cookies

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through browser settings. You can typically choose to:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject all cookies
  • Receive notification when a cookie is being set
  • Delete cookies from your device

Please note that disabling cookies may limit your ability to use certain features of our Services. Some cookies are essential for security and functionality and cannot be disabled without affecting the operation of our Services.

4.5 Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" feature. Our Services do not currently respond to Do Not Track signals because there is no industry-wide standard for recognizing Do Not Track requests.

6. Data Security

We implement comprehensive technical, administrative, and physical safeguards designed to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, and destruction. These measures include:

  • Encryption of data in transit and at rest using industry-standard protocols

  • Access controls and authentication mechanisms
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Employee training and confidentiality agreements
  • Firewalls and intrusion detection systems
  • Secure backup and disaster recovery procedures

While we implement robust security measures, no system is completely secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your information. You are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of your login credentials and account information.
